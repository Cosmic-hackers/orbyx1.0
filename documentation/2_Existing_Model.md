# 2. Existing Model

Presently, individuals or organizations looking to track orbital bodies rely heavily on platforms like Heavens-Above, N2YO, or the raw datasets provided by Space-Track and Celestrak. While highly precise and reliable, these legacy systems suffer from varying degrees of user experience shortcomings.

## 2.1 Limitations of Current Tracking Systems
- **Outdated Interface:** Many of the original tracking systems were built in the early 2000s and feature static HTML pages. They are largely non-responsive and fail to scale gracefully on modern mobile displays.
- **Page Reload Dependency:** When searching a catalog or updating a ground station on traditional sites, the entire application typically requires a full page refresh, breaking user immersion.
- **Overwhelming UI:** Scientific platforms often overwhelm users with tabular data (Inclination, RAAN, Eccentricity) taking precedence over immediately useful visual indicators (e.g., 'Where is this satellite right now over my house?').
- **Lack of Aesthetic Engagement:** The aesthetic of current tools strictly follows academic or utilitarian forms. They do not invoke the inspiring "Mission Control" feeling that attracts enthusiasts to the space sector initially.
